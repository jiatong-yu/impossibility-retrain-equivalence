Key highlights

Theory

In two-stage linear settings, path-oblivious local unlearning cannot ensure retrain equivalence across all training orders.

Recency effect

Unlearning is slowest when the forget set was learned most recently (larger performance impact for earlier-stage forget sets).

Depth of forgetting

Superficial vs. deep forgetting depends on the training path, not just the data.

Abstract (short)

We examine whether local unlearning can be retrain-equivalent in staged pipelines. Our analysis and empirical study reveal strong history dependence: two models trained on the same datasets but in different stage orders can diverge substantially under the same local unlearning procedure. This yields a practical recency effect and path-dependent depth of forgetting.
